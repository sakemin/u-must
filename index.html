Unified Music Translation between Score Images, MusicXML, MIDI, and Audio

Jongmin Jung1, Dongmin Kim1, Sihun Lee1, Seola Cho2, Hyungjoon Soh3, Irmak Bukey4, Chris Donahue4 and Dasaem Jeong2

1Department of Artificial Intelligence, Sogang University, Seoul, Republic of Korea, 2Department of Art & Technology, Sogang University, Seoul, Republic of Korea
3Department of Physics Education, Seoul National University, Seoul, Republic of Korea, 4Computer Science Department, Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh, United States

Abstract

Music exists in various modalities, such as score images, symbolic scores, MIDI, and audio. Translations between each modality are established as core tasks of music information retrieval, such as automatic music transcription (audio-to-MIDI) and optical music recognition (score image to symbolic score). However, most past work on multimodal translation trains specialized models on individual translation tasks. Here, we propose a unified approach, where we train a general-purpose model on many translation tasks simultaneously. The viability of this approach is enabled by our newly presented 1,300 hours of paired audio-score image data collected from YouTube videos, which is an order of magnitude larger than any existing music modal translation datasets. By tokenizing continuous modalities like score images and audio using discrete autoencoders, we treat modal translation between images, music notation, MIDI, and audio as a discrete seq2seq problem and train a single encoder-decoder Transformer model. Our experimental results demonstrate that a unified model trained on multiple translation tasks outperforms task-specific models. Notably, our approach achieves the first successful score-image-conditioned audio generation, marking a significant breakthrough in cross-modal music generation. Additionally, our model achieves state-of-the-art performance in optical music recognition, showing the effectiveness of our unified framework.

Inference Examples

Our multi-task, multimodal models successfully perform translation between four different modalities of music representation (score images, MusicXML, MIDI, and performance audio). Notably, we are the first to achieve end-to-end score-image-to-audio translation. As each modality highlight different aspects of music understanding, we find that the information learned for one translation task boosts performance for others.

Out of the many possible directions of modal translation, we showcase inference results for four notable ones below.

  • Image-to-Audio (I2A)
  • Multi Stage Image-to-Audio (OMR -> M2A)
  • Optical Music Recognition (OMR)
  • MIDI-to-Audio (M2A)
  • Automatic Music Transcription (AMT)
  • Audio-to-Image (A2I)
  • Image-to-Audio (I2A) in All Genres
